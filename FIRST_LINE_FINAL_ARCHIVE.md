# First Line Final Archive

## What this line tried to solve
This line focused on the low-frequency selective false-break system. The central problem was weak entry precision and long-tail dependence inside already-admitted trades. The work progressed from structural Layer 1 and Layer 2 audits into fragile-entry labeling, preserve-vs-suppress ranking, and finally narrow preserve-side refinement.

## How the line evolved
The research first tested box geometry, anchor behavior, reversal confirmation richness, volume, and entry-edge quality. That led to fragile trade classification and then to separate preserve and suppress scoring. The only direction that produced a durable research improvement was narrow preserve-side refinement that removed a small hopeless tail without removing fragile high-upside trades in-sample.

## Final best refined result
The best final research result was the refined preserve variant:
- total return about 1.0409 versus control about 0.9471
- Sharpe about 5.547 versus control about 5.048
- max drawdown about -0.1260 versus control about -0.1486
- trade count about 110 versus control about 117
- removed 7 fragile hopeless, 1 fragile mixed, and 0 fragile high-upside trades

## Why it was not promoted
The improvement was real, but still too narrow and too impure for promotion. Preserve-high purity improved only to about 0.389, which is not clean enough to support a promotion-readiness audit. The line proved that small edge compression was possible, but it did not produce a strong enough preserve-side rule to justify adoption.

## Why the line is frozen now
This line has reached diminishing returns. Additional preserve-side grinding is unlikely to create a materially cleaner result without reopening the same narrow optimization loop. The correct next step is not more tuning here. The correct next step is a separate 1H opportunity-expansion and location-edge research line.

## What future threads should inherit
Future threads should inherit the frozen production baseline, the first-line conclusion that preserve refinement helped but stopped short of promotion, and the discipline of keeping ATR, anchor, reversal, and weak volume features alive as observation variables.

## What future threads should not inherit
Future threads should not reopen broad recovery expansion, should not treat the research-best preserve variant as production, and should not continue this preserve-side line unless it is explicitly reopened for a specific reason.
